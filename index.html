About

Science at the interface of
analytics and discovery

I am currently completing my Ph.D. in the Mass Spectrometry and Proteomics Facility of the Max-Planck Institute of Biophysics in Frankfurt (expected defense May 2026), under the supervision of Dr. Julian Langer. My doctoral project focuses on the development and implementation of LC-HRMS methods to identify and quantify small proteins — an understudied class with significant biological relevance.

Throughout my Ph.D., I have collaborated on multiple parallel projects across international and interdisciplinary groups, contributing to six scientific publications. My analytical toolkit spans untargeted (DDA and DIA) and targeted (PRM) LC-HRMS workflows, combined with self-developed downstream analysis pipelines in R integrating diverse bioinformatics packages.

Before my doctoral studies, I spent over two years as an Analytical Development Analyst at Hipolabor Farmacêutica in Brazil, where I developed and validated HPLC-PDA and LC-MS/MS methods in a GMP-regulated environment, prepared regulatory documentation, and mentored junior colleagues. An earlier internship at Merck Group in Darmstadt introduced me to the international pharmaceutical R&D landscape and fueled my drive to return to industry.
