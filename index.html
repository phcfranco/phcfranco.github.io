About

Solving complex analytical challenges in
Proteomics & Drug Development

I am a pharmaceutical scientist completing a Ph.D. in Biochemistry at the Max-Planck Institute of Biophysics in Frankfurt, with a background that spans analytical method development, quantitative data analysis, and drug development across academic and industrial settings on two continents.

My scientific career began during my undergraduate studies in Pharmacy, where I accumulated research experience across analytical development, organic synthesis, and molecular biology, complemented by a funded exchange year in Ireland and an internship at Merck in Darmstadt, where I first encountered the complexity of analytical development within a large pharmaceutical organization. During my studies, I also contributed to a national project to update the Brazilian Pharmacopoeia, an experience that exposed me to the regulatory and scientific standards that surround drug quality.

After completing a Master's in Pharmaceutical Sciences, focused on the development of a new drug candidate, I transitioned into industry, where I spent over two years as an Analytical Development Analyst at a GMP-regulated pharmaceutical company in Brazil. There, I was responsible for developing and validating HPLC and LC-MS/MS methods, managing project timelines, and serving as the analytical interface between internal teams and external stakeholders including health authorities. Working through the COVID pandemic in a hospital drug supply environment taught me how scientific priorities shift under real operational pressure, and how structured analytical thinking can support critical business decisions.

I then moved to Germany to pursue my PhD, where I seek to find small proteins that had long been overlooked due to the technical challenges of detecting and quantifying them. My project sits at the intersection of analytical innovation and biological discovery, developing LC-HRMS strategies to identify and quantify these elusive targets in complex biological matrices. Beyond the experimental work, I have built quantitative data analysis pipelines in R, applied AI-based approaches to extract patterns from large datasets, and collaborated with research groups in the USA and Italy.

Across these experiences, what has remained constant is an interest in using rigorous quantitative methods to answer questions that matter, whether in quality control, drug candidate characterization, or fundamental biology. I am comfortable working at the boundary between experimental science and data analysis, and I am drawn to roles where those two worlds connect.
